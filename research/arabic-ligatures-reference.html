Arabic Ligatures & Letter Forms

Arabic letters change shape depending on their position in a word. Some letter combinations produce ligatures — merged forms that look dramatically different from their components. This guide covers every form you'll encounter as a reader.

Foundations
The 28 Letters in All Positions
Every Arabic letter has up to four forms: isolated, initial (start of word), medial (middle), and final (end). Six letters (د ذ ر ز و ا) don't connect to the left, so they lack medial and initial forms — they break the connection.
Essential
Mandatory Ligatures
These combinations are rendered as a single glyph in every Arabic font — they're not optional. The lam-alif family is the most important ligature in Arabic.
Notable
Special Word Ligatures
Some fonts render entire common words or phrases as unified calligraphic ligatures. Amiri is the most aggressive — it produces ligatures that other fonts don't. These appear primarily in religious and formal texts.
الله
Allāh
/ʔal.laːh/
The lām is doubled (shadda) and "dark" (velarized). The 'a' sounds deep, like "aw". Amiri renders the full calligraphic form with a superscript alif (dagger alif) over the lām.
لله
li-llāh (for God)
/lil.laːh/
The preposition لِ (li, "for/to") attached to الله. The initial alif of الله drops. Amiri renders as a single glyph.
بسم الله
bismillāh
/bis.mil.laːh/
"In the name of God." بِاسم contracts to بِسم — the alif of اسم drops after a preposition. Three clitics fused into one breath.
ﷺ
ṣallā allāhu ʿalayhi wa-sallam
/sˤal.laː ʔal.laː.hu ʕa.laj.hi wa.sal.lam/
"May God bless him and grant him peace." A single Unicode codepoint (U+FDFA) for the entire honorific phrase. Rendering varies hugely by font.
الرحمن
al-raḥmān (The Merciful)
/ʔar.raħ.maːn/
The lām assimilates to rā' (sun letter): الر → "ar-r". The ح is the deep pharyngeal 'h'. Note the lam-rā connection and long ā before final nūn.
محمد
Muḥammad
/mu.ħam.mad/
Dense mīm-ḥā-mīm cluster with doubled mīm (shadda). The ح is the pharyngeal, not a simple 'h'. Aggressive fonts merge the three similar-height letters tightly.

Print Amiri

Based on the Bulaq press tradition (19th-century Egypt). The most elaborate ligatures of any Google Font — produces Allah and lillah ligatures automatically. Closest to printed Arabic books.

Confusable Letter Pairs
