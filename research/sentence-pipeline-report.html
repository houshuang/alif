01Pipeline Overview

Each Arabic sentence passes through 7 stages — from word selection through LLM generation, deterministic validation, quality review, mapping verification, storage, and ongoing lifecycle management. A sentence that fails any gate is retried up to 7 times with feedback-driven correction.

Design Principle: Generate-Then-Write

All generation functions avoid holding SQLite write locks during slow LLM calls (15-30s). The pattern: Phase 1 read DB and close connection → Phase 2 call LLM (no DB lock) → Phase 3 reopen DB briefly for writes. This prevents "database is locked" cascades during concurrent access.

02LLM Generation

Sentences are generated by an LLM constrained to the learner's known vocabulary. Three generation modes exist: single-word (on-demand), batch (multiple sentences per word), and multi-target (sentences covering 2-4 words simultaneously).

System Prompt: Arabic Style Rules

ARABIC_STYLE_RULES — Grammar & Naturalness Constraints
Arabic naturalness rules: - Mix VSO and SVO word order. VSO is more formal/classical; SVO more contemporary - VSO agreement: verb matches person + gender only, NOT number: ذَهَبَ الطُّلَّابُ - SVO agreement: verb matches person + gender + number: الطُّلَّابُ ذَهَبُوا - Mix nominal sentences (descriptions/states) with verbal sentences (actions/events) - NO copula: never insert هُوَ/هِيَ as "is" with indefinite predicates. Write مُحَمَّدٌ طَبِيبٌ NOT مُحَمَّدٌ هُوَ طَبِيبٌ - Separator pronoun (ضمير الفصل) ONLY when both subject AND predicate are definite: مُحَمَّدٌ هُوَ المُدِيرُ - Idafa: first noun has NO ال and NO tanween: كِتَابُ الطَّالِبِ - Correct i'rab: nominative ضمة, accusative فتحة, genitive كسرة. Tanween on indefinites. - Use connectors naturally: و (and), فَ (so/immediately), ثُمَّ (then/after delay), لَكِنَّ (but) - Vary sentence length — mix short and long clauses - Do NOT translate English syntax literally into Arabic Sentence completeness — CRITICAL: - NEVER start a nominal sentence with an indefinite noun (نكرة). Use definite or verb-first: ✓ الوَلَدُ يَلْعَبُ (The boy plays) — definite subject ✓ يَلْعَبُ وَلَدٌ فِي الحَدِيقَةِ (A boy plays in the park) — verb-first OK with indefinite ✓ هُنَاكَ وَلَدٌ فِي الحَدِيقَةِ (There is a boy in the park) — existential OK ✗ وَلَدٌ يَلْعَبُ (A boy plays) — bare indefinite start, sounds like a caption ✗ مَطْبَخٌ وَاسِعٌ وَنَافِذَةٌ هُنَا (A kitchen and a window here) — catalog fragment - Every sentence must express a complete thought — an action, state, or relation. Avoid catalog-style adjective stacking or noun lists. Redundant pronouns — CRITICAL: - In VSO order, the verb conjugation already encodes the subject. Do NOT add a pronoun: ✓ ذَهَبَتْ إِلَى المَدْرَسَةِ (She went to school) ✗ ذَهَبَتْ هِيَ إِلَى المَدْرَسَةِ (She went she to school — redundant) - Use explicit pronouns only for emphasis/contrast: ذَهَبَ هُوَ وَلَمْ تَذْهَبْ هِيَ Semantic coherence in compound sentences: - When joining clauses with و/ثُمَّ/لَكِنَّ, they MUST be logically related: ✓ فِي الكُوَيْتِ مَطَرٌ فَأَخَذْتُ مِظَلَّةً (In Kuwait it is raining, so I took an umbrella) ✗ فِي الكُوَيْتِ مَطَرٌ وَالبَاصُ بَعِيدٌ (In Kuwait it is raining, and the bus is far — unrelated) - Do NOT combine unrelated facts into one sentence
DIFFICULTY_STYLE_GUIDE — Level-Appropriate Style
Style by difficulty level: - very simple / simple: prefer SVO. Short nominal sentences. Basic connectors (و). Simple tenses. No embedded clauses. Only modern everyday vocabulary. - beginner: mix SVO and VSO. Simple idafa. Introduce فَ and ثُمَّ. One clause per sentence. Do NOT use classical/archaic particles (لَعَلَّ، كَأَنَّ، إِذَا، لَوْلَا، حَيْثُ). Do NOT use overly formal register (يا سادة، أيها). Keep language modern and conversational. - intermediate: more VSO. Relative clauses (الَّذِي/الَّتِي). Negation with لَمْ/لَنْ. Idafa chains. Dialogue with قَالَ. - advanced: VSO default. Embedded clauses. Classical particles (إِنَّ، لَعَلَّ، كَأَنَّ). Formal register approaching classical style.

Known Words Sampling

The learner's vocabulary is sampled with inverse-frequency weighting to promote diversity:

weight = max(0.05, 1.0 / (1 + sentence_count))
jittered = weight × random.uniform(0.5, 1.5)

Words appearing in many existing sentences get lower probability. KNOWN_SAMPLE_SIZE = 500 words are sent to the LLM (up from the original 50 — this single change improved compliance by +31pp).

The target word itself is excluded from the sample to prevent self-referential examples.

Overuse Prevention

Words appearing in ≥ max(median × 1.5, 3) sentences are flagged as overused. The top MAX_AVOID_WORDS = 30 are injected into the prompt as "do NOT use". Common proper names (محمد، أحمد، فاطمة، علي) are always avoided.

Retry Loop

Feedback-Driven Retry (MAX_RETRIES = 7)

Each retry includes the previous failure reason in the prompt:

  • Validation failure: "Unknown words: X, Y — use only vocabulary list words"
  • Diversity failure: "For diversity, avoid using: X, Y — they appear in too many sentences"
  • Quality gate rejection: "Quality review rejected: {reason}. Generate a more natural sentence."

Temperature is 0.5 (some variation without randomness). All retries are logged to data/logs/sentence_gen_*.jsonl.

03Deterministic Validation

Every generated sentence passes through a fully deterministic validation pipeline — no LLM involved. A sentence is valid iff the target word is found AND there are zero unknown words.

Three-Pass Lemma Lookup

The function build_lemma_lookup() constructs a dictionary mapping normalized Arabic forms to lemma IDs. Three passes, highest priority first:

Pass 1 — Direct Bare Forms

Register each lemma's normalized bare form (diacritics stripped, alef normalized). Also registers ال-prefix variants.

Pass 2 — Forms from forms_json

Indexes ALL string-valued keys from the lemma's forms_json (no hardcoded whitelist). Includes past_3fs, past_3p, imperative, passive_participle, sound plurals, etc.

Pass 3 — Algorithmic Conjugation Generation

For each verb with present in forms_json, generates ~36 conjugation forms:

  • Past tense: 9 suffixes (ت, ا, تا, وا, ن, تُ, تَ, تِ, نا) applied to past_3ms stem
  • Present tense: 4 prefixes (ي, ت, أ, ن) × 6 suffix variants = 24 forms
  • Weak verb support: uses past_1s from forms_json for irregular stems (قال→قلت, مشى→مشيت)

For nouns: generates sound plurals (ـات، ـون، ـين) and dual forms (ـان، ـين).

Clitic Stripping

Arabic clitics (prefixed/suffixed particles) are stripped combinatorially to find the base word:

Proclitics (prefixes)وال، بال، فال، لل، كال، و، ف، ب، ل، ك
Enclitics (suffixes)هما، هم، هن، ها، كم، كن، نا، ني، ه، ك

Taa marbuta restoration: مدرسته → مدرسة + ه (converts ت back to ة when suffix removed).

All combinations are tested. Clitic-derived mappings are tagged via_clitic=True for extra scrutiny during verification.

Other Checks

  • Function word detection: ~80 particles/prepositions/pronouns/conjunctions from FUNCTION_WORD_GLOSSES, plus conjugated forms (كانت→كان, etc.)
  • Tanwin-alif stripping: سعيدًا → سعيد (removes trailing alif seat of fathatan)
  • Collision tracking: when two lemmas normalize to the same key, hamza-sensitive resolution picks the exact match; CAMeL Tools morphological analysis as fallback
Validation Result
valid = target_found AND len(unknown_words) == 0

Every token must be classified as: target word, known word, or function word. Any unrecognized token fails the sentence.

04Quality Gates

Three LLM-powered gates run after deterministic validation passes: scaffold diversity, cross-model quality review, and word-lemma mapping verification.

Gate 1: Scaffold Diversity

Rejects sentences where more than 1 scaffold word (non-target, non-function) appears in DIVERSITY_SENTENCE_THRESHOLD = 10 or more existing sentences. Prevents the same scaffold words from dominating the sentence pool.

On failure: retry feedback includes "For diversity, avoid using: [words]".

Gate 2: Quality Review

Cross-Model Review — Fail-Closed

A different model (Claude Haiku) reviews what Gemini generated. Temperature 0.0 for deterministic judgment.

Quality Review Prompt — Full Text
System: You are an expert Arabic linguist reviewing sentences for a language learning app. Focus on grammar correctness and translation accuracy. Accept simple or textbook-style sentences — they are appropriate for learners. Only reject sentences with clear errors. User: Review each Arabic sentence for a language learning app. For each: 1. Is the Arabic grammatically correct and comprehensible? 2. Is the English translation accurate? Only reject sentences with: - Grammar errors (wrong gender agreement, incorrect verb forms, broken syntax) - Translation errors (English doesn't match Arabic meaning, singular/plural mismatch) - Nonsensical or incomprehensible meaning (word salad, contradictory clauses) Do NOT reject sentences just because: - The scenario is unusual (a boy putting a book in a kitchen is fine) - The style is simple or textbook-like (these are for language learners) - Word choices are slightly informal or formal Respond with JSON array: [{"id": 1, "natural": true/false, "translation_correct": true/false, "reason": "..."}] Sentences: 1. Arabic: {arabic} English: {english} ...

Gate 3: Mapping Verification

LLM Mapping Verification — Fail-Closed

Every word-to-lemma mapping is verified against sentence context. Claude Haiku primary, Gemini fallback. Returns None on total failure (sentence rejected, NOT treated as verified).

Mapping Verification Prompt — Full Text
System: You are an Arabic morphology expert. Check each mapping against the English translation. Flag any mapping where the gloss doesn't fit the sentence meaning. User: Arabic sentence: {arabic_text} English translation: {english_text} Word-to-lemma mappings: 0: يَذْهَبُ → ذَهَبَ (to go) 1: الوَلَدُ → وَلَد (boy) 2: إِلَى → (function word) 3: المَدْرَسَةِ → مَدْرَسَة (school) [via clitic stripping] Your task: check that each word's lemma MAKES SENSE in context. Flag as WRONG (and provide correction): - The lemma's English gloss doesn't match what the word means in this sentence - Homograph collisions: same consonants but different meanings depending on voweling (e.g. جَدّ "grandfather" vs جِدّ "seriousness", عِلم "knowledge" vs عَلَم "flag") - A verb mapped to an unrelated noun or vice versa sharing consonants - A clitic prefix (و/ف/ب/ل/ك) wrongly stripped from a word where the letter is part of the root (e.g. وَصْف "description" stripped to صف "row/class") - An active participle / verbal noun mapped to the root verb when it should be its own lemma - A noun/verb homograph mapped to the wrong part of speech Do NOT flag (these are CORRECT): - A conjugated verb mapped to its dictionary form, when the MEANING matches - A plural/feminine/dual form mapped to its base lemma - A noun with possessive suffix mapped to the base noun - A word with preposition prefix where the base word is correct Words marked [via clitic stripping] had a prefix/suffix removed — higher risk for errors. When in doubt, flag it — a false positive just causes a retry, but a false negative reaches the user. Return JSON: {"issues": []} if all correct, or: {"issues": [{"position": <int>, "correct_lemma_ar": "...", "correct_gloss": "...", "correct_pos": "noun/verb/adj/etc", "explanation": "..."}]}

Gate 4: Disambiguation (Ambiguous Mappings)

When a token has multiple candidate lemma IDs (collision or multi-clitic interpretation), disambiguate_mappings_llm() presents the candidates to Claude Haiku with sentence context and asks it to pick the correct one.

05Tier-Based Lifecycle

Sentences are allocated and retired based on how urgently their target word needs review. Words due sooner get more sentences; words due later have sentences actively retired. This keeps the pool size bounded by review demand (~600-800), not vocabulary size.

TierDue WindowTarget SentencesFloor (Protected)Behavior
Tier 1≤ 12h32Highest priority backfill
Tier 212–36h21Standard generation
Tier 336–72h10Minimal coverage
Tier 4> 72h00Actively retired
Retirement Logic
  • Tier-4 excess: shown sentences immediately retired; never-shown sentences retired after 24h
  • Scaffold staleness: all scaffold words fully known (no acquiring words), shown ≥1 time → retire
  • Safety valve cap: 2,000 sentences (should never bind with tier system active)
Tier Boundary Rationale

Chosen so the cron (every 3h) has multiple cycles to fill tier 3 before words become tier 2, and tier 2 before they become tier 1. The 12h/36h/72h boundaries give 4×/12×/24× the cron period.

06Background Jobs

Cron: update_material.py (every 3 hours)

  1. Step 0 — Enforce cap: Retire excess sentences down to 2000 − 50 = 1950. Respects tier floor per word.
  2. Step A — Backfill by tier: Compute tiers, identify words below target. Multi-target generation first (groups of 2-4), single-target fallback. Uses claude_sonnet (free).
  3. Step B — Generate audio: ElevenLabs TTS for eligible sentences (word reviews ≥ 3, stability ≥ 3d). Limited batch size.
  4. Step E — Enrich lemmas: Batch enrichment for unenriched lemmas (forms_json, etymology, transliteration).

warm_sentence_cache (after every session load)

  1. Phase 1: Rotate stale sentences (tier-4 excess, scaffold staleness)
  2. Phase 2: Identify gap words — focus cohort below tier target, auto-intro candidates with < 3 sentences, recency-exhausted words (all shown in 24h)
  3. Phase 3: Generate & store sentences (multi-target + single-target)
  4. Phase 4: Batch verification — verify up to 20 unverified sentences per run. Corrects wrong mappings; retires unfixable sentences. Stamps mappings_verified_at.

10Known Limitations

Weak Verb Irregular Stems

Pass 3 uses past_1s from forms_json for irregular stems, but only for verbs where this field exists. Verbs without enrichment fall back to regular patterns, producing wrong conjugations for hollow/defective verbs.

Noun Inflection Coverage

Sound plurals (ـات/ـون/ـين) and dual forms are generated, but broken plurals and irregular patterns are only recognized via forms_json (requires LLM enrichment to have been run).

Legacy Unverified Sentences

~136 sentences from before the March 21 pipeline unification remain unverified. Background Phase 4 catches up at 20 per session, but full backfill takes time.

Corpus Viability at Scale

At current vocabulary (~700 words), corpus pass rates are still too low for primary use. BAREC becomes meaningful at ~1,000 words. Full corpus integration deferred until then.

Critical Invariants
  1. Every sentence_word must have a lemma_id — all 5 storage paths reject unmapped words
  2. Verification failure ≠ success — None return from verification rejects the sentence
  3. No auto-created lemmas — corrections only use existing DB lemmas; unfixable sentences retired
  4. No LLM in session build — all generation happens at creation time or background Phase 4
  5. Generate-then-write — never hold DB locks during LLM calls
  6. Quality gate fail-closed — LLM failure rejects the sentence, not passes it
