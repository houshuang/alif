The Problem

Interactive mobile review (tapping words, rating cards) works well at a desk but poorly on a walk. The Michel Thomas method succeeds because it manages your memory without requiring interaction — you just listen. Alif knows exactly which words you know, which you're acquiring, and how stable each one is. Can we use that data to generate personalized audio episodes?

Core insight: Alif has the SRS data that audio courses lack. Michel Thomas builds memory management into the audio timeline by hand. We can do it algorithmically — selecting sentences and repetition patterns based on actual FSRS stability, acquisition state, and root/pattern relationships.

Established Audio Methods

Michel Thomas Method Semi-Interactive

Teacher + 2 students. Progressive build-up: start with one word, add elements. "Don't try to remember." Organic recycling of structures. ~200-400 words per 8-12h course. Strength: structural competence, low anxiety. Weakness: requires mental construction during pauses, limited vocabulary.

Pimsleur Interactive

Exactly 30 minutes. Graduated Interval Recall (GIR): items return at 5s→25s→2min→10min→1day. L1 prompt → pause → produce L2 → confirmation. ~8-12 new words/lesson. Strength: the only method with formal SRS baked into audio. Weakness: requires speaking aloud.

Assimil (Passive Wave) Passive

Two-phase: 50 lessons passive (just listen/understand), then active (production). Short dialogues, line-by-line bilingual. ~2,000-3,000 words by completion. Strength: genuinely passive for first 50 lessons, excellent for walking. Weakness: no personalization, fixed progression.

Glossika Semi-Interactive

Mass sentence repetition with SRS scheduling. L1 → pause → L2 → pause → repeat. 50 sentences/day. Strength: systematic sentence exposure. Weakness: boring, no narrative, no vocabulary control.

Comprehensible Input Podcasts Passive

InnerFrench, Dreaming Spanish. Mostly/entirely L2, calibrated by level. Interesting topics, no interaction. Strength: engaging, maximum L2 exposure. Weakness: no personalization, no memory management.

Gap in the market: No existing method combines formal SRS (Pimsleur's GIR) with genuinely passive listening (Assimil/CI) and personalized vocabulary (only Alif has this). We can fill this gap.

What Research Says

Comprehensibility Threshold

Webb & Chang (2015): vocabulary acquisition from listening requires 95-98% known words. Alif's reading mode uses 60% — listening needs to be much tighter (90-95%).

Within-Session Repetition

Nakata (2017): 5 retrievals per word significantly outperforms 1 or 3. Sweet spot is 3-5 per session. Expanding intervals (1, 3, 8 items apart) slightly preferred.

Narrative Superiority

Hasson et al. (2012): stories activate more brain regions. Material with emotional valence retained ~25% better. Prediction drives encoding (testing effect).

Production vs. Passive

MacLeod et al. (2010): saying words aloud helps ~15-20% vs. passive hearing. Moderate effect — passive listening works, production is a bonus, not a requirement.

New word budget: Research + established methods converge on 5-8 new words per 30 minutes, each heard in 3-5 contexts with 3+ retrieval opportunities. Pimsleur does 8-12 but with required production. Conservative for passive: 5-8.

Seven Format Designs

Each designed for 30 minutes of passive listening, no phone interaction required.

Format 1

Sentence Drill (Glossika-style)

Pure sentence-based review with Pimsleur-style graduated recall. Each sentence: Arabic slow → English → Arabic normal. Then echo pass (Arabic only), then recall pass (English → pause → Arabic).

00:00-01:00 Opening + orientation 01:00-06:00 Block A — 5 sentences (introduce) 06:00-07:30 Block A echo (Arabic only) 07:30-12:30 Block B — 5 sentences 12:30-14:00 Block A recall (English → pause → Arabic) 14:00-20:00 Block C + echoes 20:00-26:00 Interleaved recall from all blocks 26:00-28:30 Full Arabic stream (no English) 28:30-30:00 Vocabulary summary + preview

Format 2 ★

Story Breakdown (Michel Thomas meets Audiobook)

A short story taught sentence by sentence with build-up technique. English sentence → Arabic chunks → full Arabic. After every 2-3 sentences, replay the growing sequence. Three full replays at the end.

Format 7 ★

Hybrid / Layered (Recommended Production Format)

Five-layer structure combining the best of all formats. Warm-up → vocabulary preview with root connections → story core → sentence drills → immersion close.

ElevenLabs Technical Details

Model Limits

ModelMax Chars~Duration
Flash v2.540,000~40 min
Multilingual v210,000~10 min
v35,000~5 min

A 30-min podcast needs 3+ requests with Multilingual v2. Segments stitched with pydub.

Request Stitching

Use previous_request_ids (up to 3) for prosody continuity between segments. Arabic and English generated separately to prevent accent bleed. Same voice (Chaouki) for both.

SSML Support

<break time="2.0s"/> works on Multilingual v2. Max 3s per break. Excessive use causes artifacts. Better to generate silence with pydub.

Speed Control

Range: 0.7 (learner) to 1.2 (fast). Arabic learner mode: 0.75 + comma insertion every 2 words. English narration: 1.0.
