# Arabic Media in Modern Standard Arabic (MSA) — Guide for Learners

*Research date: 2026-03-08*

## The MSA vs Dialect Reality

Almost all Arabic movies and TV dramas are produced in dialect (Egyptian, Lebanese, Syrian, Gulf, etc.). The major exception is **historical/Islamic series**, which use formal MSA because the characters are historical figures speaking in a pre-dialect era. Documentaries (especially Al Jazeera) also use MSA.

## Historical Series in MSA

### Omar (عمر) — Top recommendation
- 30 episodes about Omar ibn al-Khattab, produced by MBC (2012)
- Beautifully spoken MSA, high production value, directed by Hatem Ali
- Free with English subtitles on [Internet Archive](https://archive.org/details/OmarSeriesArabicWithEnglishSubtitles) and [bilibili](https://www.bilibili.tv/en/video/4787221641958912)
- Probably the single best MSA viewing resource available

### The Message (الرسالة) — Classic film (1976)
- Moustapha Akkad's epic about early Islam
- Shot in two separate versions with different casts (Arabic and English)
- Arabic version is in MSA — [free on Internet Archive](https://archive.org/details/TheMessageArabic1976)
- Also on [Apple TV](https://tv.apple.com/gm/movie/the-message/umc.cmc.4balj1xd9flype1bkk2tl4ztq)

### Ahmad ibn Hanbal (الإمام أحمد بن حنبل)
- 31 episodes, produced by Qatar Media Foundation (2017)
- All in MSA, available on YouTube with English subtitles

### Fath al-Andalus (فتح الأندلس)
- Syrian-Kuwaiti production about the Islamic conquest of Spain
- MSA, available on YouTube

### Harun al-Rashid series
- About the Abbasid golden age, MSA

## Documentaries

- **Al Jazeera Documentary channel** (الجزيرة الوثائقية) — massive library of MSA documentaries about Arab/Islamic history, all on YouTube. Best ongoing source for MSA documentary content.
- **"The Arabs: A Living History"** — BBC co-production exploring Arab history, culture and society

## Streaming Access from Norway

| Platform | MSA Content | Subtitles |
|----------|------------|-----------|
| YouTube | Best source — Al Jazeera docs, many historical series | Often has EN subs |
| Internet Archive | Omar series, The Message | EN subs available |
| Shahid (MBC) | Some historical series | Arabic + some EN |
| Netflix | Very little MSA — mostly dialect dramas | Good subtitle support |
| WatanFlix | Arabic series and documentaries | Varies |

Netflix is poor for MSA content specifically. YouTube + Internet Archive are the best bets from Norway.

## Learning Strategy

The Omar series is ideal for MSA learners — formal but natural MSA, relatively clear and well-paced dialogue (not news-speed), and the historical context provides high-frequency vocabulary. Watch once with English subtitles, then rewatch without.
